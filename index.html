Sorting Algorithms

Insertion Sort

Insertion sort creates new elements that are not related to the data set and randomly inserts them, thus confusing many people who study the set.

Quick Sort

Quick sort is the slowest sort ever offered, approximately O(100000N^2).

Merge Sort

Merge sort chooses another random data set and merges the values with yours. Good luck distinguishing your important numbers from the incoming cat temperature values!

Selection Sort

Selection sort selects its favorite elements and discards the rest. Who needs them, anyway?

Shell Sort

Shell sort puts the elements into crabs' shells and hopes that the crabs put them in order.
